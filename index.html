Nearly 5000 retail stores will be closed in 2019. But, many retail workers are here to stay

The announced shutdown of 2,500 stores by Payless Shoe Source will lead to loss of 18,000 jobs. Gymboree Group Inc., a children apparel retailer will shut operations of two of its subsidiaries, effectively terminating jobs of 9,600 employees.

Even as many blue-collar workers lose their jobs, economists believe they are less affected than white-collar workers. There is enough fluidity in the retail sector for a blue-collared worker to be rehired again by another retail store. In the past months, many retail giants have actually made big moves to attract these laid-off employees.

For example, Walmart now offers its employees bonuses for good attendance and Target recently raised its minimum wage to $13.

“This is not the end of retail,” said Mike Montgomery, Senior Economist, IHS Markit. “The market is so fluid that you have people moving from one store to another.”

However, it has been more difficult for a white-collar worker to find a job. “That’s the point of mergers and acquisitions to reduce white collar jobs and become more efficient,” says Montgomery.

Andrew Zatlin, founder of SouthBay Research says that blue-collar workers have a lot of choices. “Same job, different place.”

However, when malls close the foot traffic goes to the nearby store. For example, when a Circuit City closes, people head to close by Best Buy. But, Best Buy doesn’t need more accountants or marketing workers, what they need is more floor salesperson, as per Zaitlin.
